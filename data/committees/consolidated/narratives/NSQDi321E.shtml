QDi.321 MALIK MUHAMMAD ISHAQ

Date on which the narrative summary became available on the Committee’s website: 14.03.2014

Malik Muhammad Ishaq was listed on 14 March 2014 pursuant to paragraphs 2 and 3 of resolution 2083 (2012) as being associated with Al-Qaida for “participating in the financing, planning, facilitating, preparing, or perpetrating of acts or activities by, in conjunction with, under the name of, on behalf of, or in support of”, “supplying, selling or transferring arms and related materiel to”, “recruiting for” or “otherwise supporting acts or activities of” Al-Qaida (QDe.004) and Lashkar i Jhangvi (LJ) (QDe.096).

Additional information:

Malik Muhammad Ishaq is one of the founders and a current leader of Lashkar i Jhangvi (LJ) (QDe.096). In February 2013, Pakistani police arrested Ishaq in connection with sectarian attacks in the northwestern city of Quetta, Pakistan that killed nearly 200 people on 10 January and 16 February 2013. LJ claimed responsibility for the Quetta bombing. According to Pakistani police, LJ accepted responsibility for the Quetta blast and Ishaq is LJ’s supreme commander.

LJ has conducted numerous suicide and other terror attacks inside Pakistan and Afghanistan. LJ has targeted civilians, including doctors, lawyers, teachers, politicians, lobbyists and scholars; it has also attacked targets in Pakistan on the basis of their Christian or Shia Muslim affiliation.

LJ members have trained with Al-Qaida (QDe.004) at camps in Afghanistan.

Malik Ishaq is reported to have admitted in October 1997 in an interview that he was involved in the killings of over 100 people.
